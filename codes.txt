     <div class="fakeimg" style="height:300px; opacity: 0.8; color:#fff; text-shadow: 2px 2px #000000;"><h2>“Trade has always been shaped by technology but the rapid</br>development of digital technologies in recent times has the</br>potential to transform international trade profoundly</br>in the years to come."</h2><p><i>-World Trade Report 2018 (World Trade Organization)-</i></p></div>
      <p>Information technology has changed how businesses around the world conduct foreign trade. It is not only how they communicate but also performing research, marketing products, and manage global supply chains. It has particularly opened-up opportunities for businesses of all sizes to expand the reach of their products and services, choose the best suppliers from around the globe and take advantage of advanced manufacturing solutions. For financial institutions who facilitates the trade transaction is to keep up with this trend and to provide smart solutions for their customer requirements. As an international trade finance specialist and a post graduate student in the field of IT, I would like to share some information about what role does Information Technology plays in Trade Finance sector.</p>
    </div>


    <div class="card">
      <div class="fakeimg1" style="height:200px;"><h3>Communication</h3></div>
      <p>For almost all financial institutions around the world which offer foreign trade financing products, SWIFT (Society for Worldwide Interbank Financial Telecommunication) is a key tool for communicating financial instructions and for authentication purposes. The SWIFT system is a secure, fast, and reliable communication platform that has contributed towards shrinking the use of inefficient and costly paper-based communication.</p>
      <a href="https://www.swift.com/" target="_blank">Click for more details</a><br/><br/>
      

      <div class="fakeimg2" style="height:200px;"><h3>Electronic Documents (EDMS)</h3></div>
      <p>Traders and facilitators around the world are moving towards electronic documents (E-docs) instead of using physical documents. This is mainly because they are easier to create, share, organize, and collaborate on. In international trade, companies and organizations are now encouraged to use following type of common documents electronically.
  <ul>
	<li>Commercial Invoice</li>
	<li>Packing List</li>
	<li>Bill of Lading / Air Waybills / Consignment Notes</li>
	<li>Origin and Inspection Certificates</li>
	<li>Insurance Certificate</li>
  </ul>
	<p>The financial institutions are implementing electronic document management systems to handle these E-docs and it is following benefits than using the physical documents.</p>
  <ul>
	<li>Convenient to store and create backup</li>
	<li>Easy to extract data and screen</li>
	<li>Easy to create copies and transfer</li>
	<li>Can add more protection to the transaction</li>
  </ul>
	<p>Moving to E-docs organizations can save a lot of cost related to papers, copiers, storage facilities, and mostly the cost incurred by courier/post services. Popular EDMS are <br/>
  <ul>    
    <li>Zoho Docs</li>
	<li>Logical Doc</li>
	<li>Microsoft SharePoint Online</li>
  </ul></p>
    

    <div class="fakeimg3" style="height:200px;"><h3>Real-Time Tracking & Verification</h3></div>
    <p>Thanks to the GPS and Tracking technology the traders and organizations can track the real-time movement of their shipments. Many global intelligence organizations are offering this service to their clients so that the traders can do business more confidently and the facilitators can identify any fraudulent activity and operate in safe zone. <a href="http://www.imbship.com/" target="_blank">International Maritime Bureau</a> is one of the regulating bodies that provides these services. <a href="https://www.seasearcher.com/" target="_blank">Lloyd’s List Intelligence - Seasearcher</a> is another popular option.</p><br/>
    

    <div class="fakeimg4" style="height:200px;"><h3>Data Screening & Filtering</h3></div>
    <p>Since International Trade is one big playing field for the fraudsters and money-launderers it is utmost important that trade financing institutions to conduct proper transaction screening on each transaction to make sure that their system is not being used to finance terrorists or human/drug traffickers. Not just providing a huge data base of embargo/sanctioned entities modern technology has improved these systems to identify patterns and possible matches using the AI technology. This will allow genuine traders to conduct their financial activities without any unnecessary delays occurring from manual screening. Some of the most recognized watch list and filtering solutions as follows,</p>
  <ul>
	<li><a href="https://www.wolfsberg-principles.com/articles/publication-guidance-sanctions-screening" target="_blank">Wolfsburg Group</a></li>
